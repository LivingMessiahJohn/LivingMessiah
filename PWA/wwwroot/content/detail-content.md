## Hermeneutic Exegesis not Eisegesis
> Exegesis is the scholarly process of interpreting a text, especially authoritative or sacred writings such as the Bible, by systematically analyzing its linguistic, historical, and contextual elements to discern the original author's intended meaning. 
> A practice contrasted with eisegesis, which introduces ideas into the text to align it with the interpreter's view. [Source](https://grokipedia.com/page/Exegesis)
